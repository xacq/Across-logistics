When to use ground transport

Ground transport is ideal for flexible regional, domestic and cross-border logistics.

This service is recommended when cargo must move between warehouses, ports, airports, production facilities, distribution centers or final delivery points with controlled cost and adaptable routing.

Domestic distribution

For cargo moving within the same country through road freight networks.

Cross-border movement

For international road transport requiring coordination, documentation and route planning.

Intermodal efficiency

For operations where combining road and rail can reduce costs and improve logistics performance.

Operational control

Ground transport connects the rest of the supply chain.

Road and intermodal transport are critical for linking air freight, sea freight, customs, warehousing and final distribution. Across Logistics coordinates these movements as part of an integrated logistics operation.
