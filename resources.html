FAQ

Common logistics questions.

Quick answers to help you prepare shipment, customs and transport details before contacting the team.

What information is needed to request a quote?

Origin, destination, cargo type, dimensions, weight, timing, preferred transport mode and any special handling or customs requirements.

When should I use air freight instead of sea freight?

Air freight is usually preferred for urgent, high-value or time-sensitive cargo. Sea freight is often better for larger volumes and optimized cost.

What documents are usually required for customs?

Typical documents include commercial invoice, packing list, transport document, commodity details and any certificates required by origin or destination authorities.

What is the difference between FCL and LCL?

FCL means a full container load used by one shipper. LCL means less-than-container-load cargo consolidated with shipments from other customers.

Why is cargo insurance important?

Cargo insurance helps protect against financial loss from covered transport risks and supports better risk management for international shipments.

When is temperature controlled logistics required?

It is required when products must remain within defined temperature ranges, such as perishables, healthcare cargo or other sensitive goods.
